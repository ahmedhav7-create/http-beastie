Ahmed Kamel 900244173 RHET1020-11 Autobiography Assignment September 18, 2025 Autobiography My name is Ahmed and I am an Egyptian citizen born on February 7, 2007 and an 18-year-old student at AUC. My mom was the one who gave me my name, alleging that she dreamed of that name shining while being pregnant with me. I used to be educated in Arabic in my school stages and in English for its subject only. Hence, I decided that I had to ameliorate my unsatisfactory linguistic skills in English and my uncle provided me with the significant assistance for that as an English expert. Moving on, I reached the stage of Thanaweya Amma and was enrolled in a government school. I endeavored to achieve my dream of being one of the top 10 students nationally. However, I couldn't attain that accomplishment and ended up becoming one of the top 500 students with a 96.6% score. Yet, I could receive a fully funded scholarship for public schools due to my diligence, determination and consistent passion for excellence and the happiness and eagerness my father showed due to that splendid news made me realize that the delight and contentment of my parents was what I should have sought not academic achievements. During my journey at AUC, I have gone through an enriching experience and gained heartwarming memories, beginning with the 0210 English course till now. 0210 was one of the most influential events in my life. It was, for instance, my first time having a presentation task, which led me to be slightly anxious and careworn. However, with the guidance of my instructor, I could overcome that obstacle. Consequently, not only did I develop my language skills, but I also acquired diverse social and communication skills. Moreover, I have built new connections with other students with similar backgrounds, who share the same traits and a common vision of achieving success. Furthermore, one of my considerable achievements in the university is working as a teaching assistant for Calculus II and it truly delights me to ease the material for other students and assist with their core concerns in that course. I am majoring in electronics and communication engineering, a field I picked selectively since it is the one most related to physics and modern technology, which are my topics of passion and interest, while also being involved as well in the discipline of engineering. Regarding my interests, chess is considered my primary one and that deep passion began when my cousin invited me to challenge him in a chess game. I was extremely enthusiastic about that challenge and concerned as well, as I had no knowledge or experience in chess at that time. However, my cousin told me the basics, moves of pieces and an initial opening for novice chess players and started playing. I could defeat my cousin quickly and that was what sparked me to learn more about this game, practice it and strive to become a professional player. I have already participated in a plethora of chess competitions, challenging opponents from various age groups, for example, the elderly, adolescents and even children. My current rating is 1500 and it is required to be above 2000 to be recognized as a professional player. Hence, I aspire to work diligently to reach that category of master players. Additionally, I began practicing table tennis on my first day at AUC. I initially had no passion for that sport, however, after being defeated by another colleague that same day at university, I was motivated to practice it more to enhance my level and refine my style of playing. Since then, I have been playing it regularly and was able to defeat that student, who was the reason for my fondness for that game, in my second semester of university. I look forward to joining the university table tennis team through competing in the coming tryouts at AUC. Therefore, my patience, logical reasoning and critical thinking have significantly developed over the five years of playing chess. Thinking of a strategy has become my priority whenever I am ordered to complete a task or face hardships in life. In addition, table tennis has strengthened my determination, leading me to become resistant to thinking about surrendering in any life situation. Thus, my hobbies have benefited me in several ways, which will contribute positively to my research process. In fact, thinking about chess as a slightly ignored science has inspired me to form my opinion to research about science and whether it still has its value or not.
